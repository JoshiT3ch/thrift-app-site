Privacy Policy

How Thrift handles account, marketplace, and safety-related data.

This Privacy Policy explains what information Thrift collects, how that information is used, when it may be shared, and the choices available to users. It is written for a marketplace app that includes listings, messaging, purchase requests, seller profiles, moderation, and user-generated content.

Who we are

Thrift is a mobile marketplace focused on preloved fashion and thrifted clothing. People use the app to create listings, browse items, save favorites, message one another, send purchase requests, view seller profiles, leave ratings where available, and coordinate meetup or delivery. This website exists to provide a public presence for the service, including legal and support information.

When we say "Thrift," "we," "our," or "us" in this Privacy Policy, we mean the operators of the Thrift platform and the systems used to run it.

Information we collect

Account information

When you create or maintain an account, we may collect information such as your name, username, email address, encrypted password credentials, profile image, city or location details, and any other profile information you choose to provide. We may also keep a record of account preferences and settings.

Listings and uploaded content

We collect the information you submit when you create or edit listings. This may include photos, item titles, descriptions, sizes, prices, brand details, condition notes, categories, tags, availability, and your stated preferences for meetup or delivery. If you upload profile images or listing media, that content is also collected and stored as part of the service.

Messages and communications

Thrift includes messaging and marketplace communication features. We collect messages sent through the app, purchase-related requests, replies, user reports, and communications sent to support. We may also collect attachments or contextual details included in those communications.

Device, usage, and log data

Like most online services, we automatically receive some technical information when you use Thrift. This may include your IP address, device type, operating system, app version, browser type when using the website, approximate location derived from IP, timestamps, crash or error data, search activity, screens viewed, referring pages, and interaction logs that help us understand how the service is performing.

Safety, verification, and support information

If you contact support, appeal a moderation action, report another user, or become part of a safety review, we may collect the information needed to investigate and respond. This can include account identifiers, the content of the report, relevant listing history, message history, and any evidence you provide.

How we use information

We use information collected through Thrift to operate, improve, and protect the platform. In practice, this includes using data to:

  • Create and manage user accounts and profiles.
  • Display listings, support search and filtering, and enable favorites and seller profile features.
  • Facilitate communication between buyers and sellers, including chat and purchase-related requests.
  • Respond to support requests, product issues, moderation appeals, and safety concerns.
  • Investigate misuse, spam, fraud, counterfeit activity, abusive conduct, or attempts to bypass platform rules.
  • Maintain system performance, troubleshoot bugs, monitor usage patterns, and improve reliability and product quality.
  • Comply with applicable laws, lawful requests, internal record-keeping needs, and enforcement obligations.

Moderation and safety use

Because Thrift is a user-driven marketplace with listings and messaging, moderation and trust systems are an important part of how the service works. We may review listings, reports, message-related records, user activity patterns, and technical signals to detect and address behavior that may violate our Terms of Service or Community Guidelines.

This review may be manual, automated, or a combination of both. We use it to detect fraud, harassment, spam, counterfeit listings, restricted items, impersonation, and other conduct that threatens user safety or platform integrity.

Sharing and third-party services

We do not sell personal information in the ordinary course of running Thrift. We may share information with service providers and infrastructure partners only as needed to operate the platform, secure the service, and support legitimate business functions.

Cloudinary

Listing images, profile images, and other uploaded media may be processed or stored through Cloudinary or related media delivery services so we can host, optimize, and serve images reliably.

Render

Our backend services may be hosted through Render, which means application traffic, service logs, and operational data may pass through infrastructure used to run and maintain the backend environment.

MongoDB and related hosting or infrastructure

Account data, listing data, messaging records, and other marketplace information may be stored in MongoDB databases and supported by related managed hosting, backup, monitoring, and security infrastructure used to keep the service available and recoverable.

Legal and protective disclosures

We may also disclose information when we believe it is reasonably necessary to comply with law, respond to valid legal process, investigate fraud or abuse, enforce our policies, protect the rights or safety of users or others, or prevent harm to the platform.

How long data may be retained

We keep information for as long as it is needed to operate the service, maintain reasonable business records, enforce platform rules, resolve disputes, and comply with legal obligations. Different categories of information may be retained for different periods depending on why they were collected and how they are used.

  • Account and profile data may be retained while your account is active and for a reasonable period after closure.
  • Listings and uploaded media may remain in system records, caches, or backups for a limited period even after they are removed from public view.
  • Messages, reports, and moderation records may be kept longer when needed to investigate abuse, protect users, or document enforcement actions.
  • Technical logs and diagnostic records may be retained for security, performance, and audit purposes.

Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, update, delete, or request a copy of certain personal information. You may also have the right to object to or limit some forms of data use where the law gives you that option.

You can update parts of your account information directly in the app where that functionality is available. For other requests, including privacy questions or deletion assistance, contact us at support@thriftapp.com. We may ask for reasonable verification before completing a request that affects account access or personal data.

Account deletion rights

Users may request or initiate account deletion from within the Thrift mobile app. If you cannot access the app, you may contact support for help. When an account deletion request is completed, we will remove or de-identify personal information where appropriate, subject to exceptions needed for fraud prevention, safety enforcement, legal compliance, dispute handling, backup recovery, or audit requirements.

Public-facing profile information and active listings are generally removed from normal display after deletion. Some records may remain in internal systems for limited periods where retention is justified for the reasons described in this policy.

Children and minimum age

Thrift is not intended for children under 13, or under the minimum age required in a user's jurisdiction to use an online marketplace without parental involvement. If we learn that personal information has been collected from a child in a way that is not permitted, we will take appropriate steps to remove that information or restrict the account as required.

Updates to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes to the product, operational practices, safety measures, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date on this page and may provide additional notice through the app or website where appropriate.
