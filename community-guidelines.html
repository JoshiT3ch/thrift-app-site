Community Guidelines

Standards for respectful, honest, and safe marketplace behavior.

Thrift works best when listings are accurate, conversations stay respectful, and users treat one another fairly. These guidelines explain the behaviors we expect and the types of conduct that can lead to enforcement.

Last updated: April 2026

On this page

Respect and honest dealing

Treat other users the way you would expect to be treated in a real-world marketplace. Be direct, clear, and truthful about your items, intentions, and availability. If a deal changes, communicate that clearly instead of disappearing or misleading the other person.

No harassment or hate

Harassment, hate speech, threats, stalking, intimidation, or discriminatory behavior have no place on Thrift. This includes abusive comments, repeated unwanted contact, targeted insults, slurs, sexual harassment, and attempts to make another user feel unsafe.

No spam

Do not flood the platform with duplicate listings, repetitive messages, promotional spam, or irrelevant content. Fake engagement, mass messaging, and manipulative attempts to game search or visibility are also prohibited.

No fraudulent listings

Listings must reflect a real item that you are genuinely offering. Do not post items you do not own, misstate condition, use bait-and-switch pricing, hide major flaws, or mislead buyers about authenticity, location, delivery, or availability.

No counterfeit items

Counterfeit, replica, or otherwise infringing items are not allowed. If a branded item cannot be honestly represented, do not list it. We may remove listings and take action on related accounts when authenticity concerns arise.

No illegal or restricted goods

Thrift is for lawful resale of fashion-related items and similar permitted goods. Illegal items, stolen goods, unsafe products, restricted goods, or listings that violate local law are prohibited. If you are unsure whether an item is allowed, do not list it until you have confirmed it is appropriate.

No misleading photos or descriptions

Use your own photos or photos you have the right to use, and make sure they represent the actual item being sold. Descriptions should be specific enough for buyers to understand the condition, fit, wear, flaws, and material details that matter. Edited or borrowed images that create the wrong impression can lead to removal.

No abusive messaging

In-app messages should stay relevant to the transaction or listing. Do not use chat to harass, pressure, threaten, scam, or manipulate other users. Keep communication respectful even when a deal does not work out.

Reporting process

If you see a listing, account, or message that appears unsafe, deceptive, unlawful, or abusive, use the reporting tools in the app or contact support. Reports help us review issues more quickly, especially when users include clear details about what happened.

Enforcement actions

We review violations based on severity, pattern, and risk to the platform. Enforcement may include one or more of the following actions:

  • Warning for lower-severity or first-time issues.
  • Listing removal when content violates platform rules.
  • Temporary restriction on posting, messaging, or account access.
  • Permanent ban for serious abuse, repeated violations, fraud, or safety threats.

Some conduct may result in immediate removal or account suspension without prior warning, especially where user safety or counterfeit activity is involved.
